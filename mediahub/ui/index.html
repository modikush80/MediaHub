  3. Plug in a source drive → Stage to Destination → check it shows connected or matched (renamed).

New here? That's the whole loop. The sections below explain each piece in more depth.

What MediaHub does

It takes a large photo/video archive scattered across external SSDs, understands it (trips, devices, RAW/JPEG, orientation), removes duplicates safely, and copies one trip at a time into a clean Location / Year / Month / raw·edited / images·videos / device structure on your SSD or NAS — resumable across drive swaps and restarts.

The core workflow — staging a trip

  1. Settings → choose your destination (Local SSD or a mounted drive/NAS). Turn on SHA-256 verify for extra safety.
  2. Stage to Destination → connect the source drive(s). Check the Source drives panel shows them as connected or matched (renamed).
  3. Pick a trip → Preview to see the exact folder tree that will be created.
  4. Dry run → plans the copy and reports counts/sizes without writing anything.
  5. Copy → confirm the sheet, then MediaHub copies (and verifies) each file. Safe to quit and resume anytime.
  6. If a needed drive isn't connected, MediaHub pauses and tells you which drive to attach next, then Continue.
  7. When the trip finishes, upload that one folder to your NAS, then use Duplicates to review what's safe to remove yourself.
Safety chain: Preview → Dry run → Confirm → Copy → Verify. A real copy can never start by accident.

Source drives & “matched (renamed)”

MediaHub recognizes a drive by its identity, not just its name — so if you renamed a drive after indexing it, it still works. The status chips mean:

  • connected — mounted under its original name, ready to stage.
  • matched (renamed) — this is a success state. MediaHub recognized the drive even though it's now mounted under a different name. Nothing more to do — it's ready to stage.
  • ambiguous — more than one drive could match; pick the right one from the dropdown and click Use this.
  • not connected — attach the drive (or pick the matching mounted volume and click Use this).
After you click Use this, the row updates to matched (renamed) — that means it worked and the drive is now linked.

On-Device Apple Intelligence

All AI runs locally on your Mac — nothing leaves the device. First enable the engines once in Semantic Search → On-device components (installs into MediaHub's own private environment — your system Python is never touched):

  • numpy — faster search ranking.
  • MLX CLIP — true visual search (match pixels, not filenames).
  • MLX-VLM — AI photo captions (descriptions).

Semantic Search — find photos by content. Build embeddings, then search “waterfall”, “sunset over water”, even with natural language like “drone shots from 2024” (it auto-applies device/year filters). Results show thumbnails with Open and Reveal in Finder. With the stub backend it matches filenames; MLX matches actual image content.

Vision & OCR — for screenshots & documents. Browse a folder → Run Vision tagging reads the text inside images and buckets them. Search that text (e.g. “rakuten cashback 10%”); click a bucket to expand thumbnails, click again to collapse.

AI captions — describe every photo for full-text search (stub works now; MLX-VLM gives real descriptions).

People — groups faces on-device into person clusters. Private; report-only.

Smart Culling — finds near-identical/burst shots, suggests one to keep, and gives a CSV plan. It only suggests — it never deletes.

New trips later

Back from a trip? New Trip Ingest → Browse → pick the folder → Scan & ingest. It folds the new media into your inventory; then stage it like any other trip. Adding new data never disturbs what's already organized. (Staged trips are also auto-embedded for search.)

Deleting files & keeping the database honest

After you back up and delete files from a drive, reconcile the database in Settings → Library maintenance:

  • Scan for deleted files — only checks files on currently-mounted drives; files on unplugged drives are never touched.
  • Remove from index → moves them to Trash (a recovery window, default 30 days). The sidebar badge shows the count.
  • Restore anything by mistake, or Empty trash to purge. This only edits database rows — your media files are never deleted by MediaHub.
  • Optional Auto-reconcile on launch toggle (off by default).

Pairing with Immich (optional)

Want a phone app, timeline, and sharing on top of this archive? In Settings → Immich external library click Prepare for Immich — it writes setup notes into your destination so Immich can read the organized folder in place (read-only). MediaHub stays your safe organizer; Immich becomes the browsing layer.

Make it yours

Settings → Appearance — pick a theme (Auto, Light, Midnight, or the refractive Aurora glass) and an accent color. Changes apply instantly and are remembered.
